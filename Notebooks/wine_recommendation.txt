# Wine & Food Pairing Recommendation

**Wine Recommendation:**  
[**Wine:** Cantine Barbera d'Asti Superiore Nizza "Bricco Fiasco" 2018**](https://www.google.com/search?q=Wine:**+Cantine+Barbera+d'Asti+Superiore+Nizza+"Bricco+Fiasco"+2018)  
**Recommendation:**

**Wine:** Cantine Barbera d'Asti Superiore Nizza "Bricco Fiasco" 2018

**Key Details:**

* **Color:** Ruby red
* **Flavor Profile:** Citrus notes, particularly grapefruit and orange, with hints of red fruit
* **Aroma:** Fragrant and fruity, with ripe red wine aromas such as plums and cherries
* **Sweetness Level:** 7 out of 10
* **Body:** Light-bodied
* **Price Range:** $25 - $40

**Description:**

This light-bodied Barbera d'Asti from Piedmont, Italy, offers a unique blend of citrusy and fruity flavors. The wine's vibrant acidity and low tannins contribute to its freshness and easy-drinking nature. The aromas of ripe red fruits provide a pleasant balance to the citrus notes, creating a complex and enjoyable wine.

<details>
  <summary><strong>Recipe & Instructions</strong></summary>
  **Mushroom Risotto with Grilled Steak**

**Pairs perfectly with:** The Cantine Barbera d'Asti Superiore Nizza "Bricco Fiasco" 2018

**Ingredients:**

**For the risotto:**
- 1 cup Arborio rice
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- ½ cup white wine (optional)
- ½ cup chopped onion
- 2 cups chopped mushrooms (any variety)
- ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley
- 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 tablespoon butter
- Salt and pepper to taste

**For the steak:**
- 1 pound flank steak or skirt steak
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
- ½ teaspoon grated garlic
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Instructions:**

**For the steak:**

1. Combine olive oil, Worcestershire sauce, garlic, salt, and pepper in a small bowl to create a marinade.
2. Brush the marinade over the steak and let it sit for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight.
3. Heat a grill or grilling pan to medium-high heat.
4. Grill the steak for 5-7 minutes per side for medium-rare, or until desired doneness is reached.
5. Let the steak rest for 10 minutes before slicing against the grain.

**For the risotto:**

1. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter and sauté the onion until softened.
2. Add the rice and stir to coat in the butter.
3. Add the white wine (if using) and let it cook off before adding the vegetable broth.
4. Simmer the risotto over low heat, stirring occasionally.
5. Once the liquid has been absorbed, add the mushrooms and parsley and cook until the mushrooms are softened.
6. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and butter.
7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

**To serve:**

1. Place the sliced steak on a plate and top with the mushroom risotto.
2. Garnish with additional parsley, if desired.
